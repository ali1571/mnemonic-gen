// THE MATH BEHIND THE COUNTER

STEP 1 — WHERE DOES THE BIG NUMBER COME FROM?

Your app generates 12-word BIP39 mnemonics. Each word is chosen from a list of 2048 words.

2048 = 211

Each word = 11 bits of information

12 words = 12 × 11 = 132 bits total

But 4 of those bits are checksum (validation, not randomness). So the actual unique entropy is:

132 - 4 = 128 bits of real randomness

That means total possible unique wallets =

2128 =

340,282,366,920,938,463,463,374,607,431,768,211,456

That's your number. 39 digits. That's how many distinct 12-word seed phrases can ever exist.

STEP 2 — WHAT IS "COLLISION"?

A collision = two different generation attempts produce the exact same wallet. If that happened, whoever generated it second could drain the first person's funds.

The question is: how likely is that? This uses the Birthday Problem from probability:

After generating N wallets,

probability of collision ≈ N² / 2129

So if you generated 1 billion wallets per second for the entire age of the universe (~14 billion years):

N = 1,000,000,000 × 60 × 60 × 24 × 365 × 14,000,000,000

N ≈ 4.4 × 1026 wallets

Collision probability ≈ (4.4×1026)² / 2129

≈ 1053 / 1039

≈ 0.000000000000 (still basically zero)

You would need to generate 264 wallets (~18 quintillion) before the collision probability even reaches 50%. That's thermodynamically impossible at current compute.
